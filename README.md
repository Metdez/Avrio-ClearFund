## The Problem

Private credit firms operate at the intersection of borrowers and capital providers — hotels, infrastructure projects, mining operations on one side; banks, asset managers, family offices, and life insurance companies on the other. Traditional CRMs collapse under the weight of multi-threaded, two-sided relationships where a single capital provider might simultaneously be evaluating three different deals, negotiating a credit facility, and exploring a joint venture.

The result? Deal teams live in spreadsheets. Context lives in someone's head. Follow-ups fall through the cracks. And millions of dollars in pipeline value evaporate because nobody remembered to send that second email.

**We built the thing that fixes all of that.**

## What Makes This Different

### Dual-Sided Relationship Intelligence

Most CRMs think in contacts. We think in **relationships**. Every capital provider has a classification — Prospective, Transactional, or Credit Facility Partner — that evolves dynamically as the relationship deepens. When a CP commits capital to their first deal, the system knows. When they sign a facility agreement, it auto-upgrades. No manual status updates. No stale data.

### Multi-Threaded Engagement Tracking

One capital provider. Five simultaneous conversations. Zero confusion.

Engagement threads let your team track Deal Evaluations, Credit Facility Negotiations, JV Partnerships, and ad-hoc discussions **independently** — each with its own status lifecycle, notes, and timeline. A unified chronological view stitches everything together so you never lose context, no matter how complex the relationship gets.

### Drag-and-Drop Pipeline That Actually Understands Your Workflow

Eight pipeline stages from **Prospect** to **Closed** — with full Kanban drag-and-drop. But this isn't a generic board. Backward moves trigger confirmation dialogs because in private credit, moving a deal from Structuring back to Qualifying means something. Moving to Execution auto-generates task checklists. The pipeline *understands the domain*.

### Capital Provider Outreach Dashboard

For every deal, see exactly where you stand with every capital provider at a glance. Six status cards — Pitched, Evaluating, Negotiating, Committed, Declined, Withdrawn — with inline note editing, status updates, and one-click navigation to provider profiles. This is how you run a capital raise, not with sticky notes.

### Credit Facility Management

Track facility size, annual allocation, spread splits, term length, and refinancing provisions with real-time utilization tracking. Progress bars turn amber at 80% and red at 100%. Your team sees remaining capacity instantly. No more accidentally over-allocating a $200M facility.

### Full Deal Execution Workspace

When a deal hits Execution, the real work begins. 8-15 third-party vendors. 6-9 weeks of coordinated tasks. Our execution workspace gives you:

- **Task checklists** auto-generated from process templates
- **Vendor management** with service type classification and deal history
- **Overdue flagging** — red indicators, "X days overdue", sort by urgency
- **Progress tracking** — color-coded bars (green/amber/red) that tell you at a glance if a deal is on track
- **Deal termination workflows** with required reason fields and full audit trails

### AI-Powered Follow-Up Automation

Set up intelligent follow-up sequences with configurable intervals, choose between auto-send and human-in-the-loop approval mode, and review AI-generated drafts before they go out. The approval queue shows full context — who, why, and what was said last — so your team makes informed decisions in seconds, not minutes.

### Unified Email Intelligence

Emails flow in from Gmail or Outlook and land in a smart queue. The system auto-suggests entity associations by matching sender addresses to your contacts. One click to confirm, and that email is permanently linked to the right borrower, capital provider, deal, and engagement thread. Full conversation threading with AI response suggestions built right in.

### Document Hub

Every term sheet, appraisal, and legal document — organized, searchable, and linked to the right entity. Drag-and-drop upload. Per-entity document sections on every profile page. Version history tracking. No more digging through shared drives.

### Process Template Engine

Define your execution playbook once. Drag-to-reorder tasks, set default assignee roles, configure relative due dates. Apply templates to new deals with one click. Version history ensures your process evolves without losing what worked before.

### Zoho CRM Migration Built In

Already in Zoho? We've got you. One-click OAuth connection, intelligent contact classification during import (is this a Borrower or a Capital Provider?), ongoing sync with conflict resolution, and a full sync log so you always know what changed and when.

## Built for Finance Professionals

This isn't a developer tool with a finance skin. Every design decision was made with **non-technical users from leveraged finance and investment backgrounds** in mind:

- **Data density over whitespace** — These users read spreadsheets for breakfast. Tables are first-class citizens, not afterthoughts.
- **Professional aesthetic** — Clean slate/green palette. No whimsy. No emoji-laden interfaces. This belongs on a trading floor.
- **Speed** — Simulated loading states are brief and purposeful. Every interaction feels immediate.
- **No hidden features** — Clear labels, obvious actions, predictable navigation. If it takes more than one click to find something, we failed.

## Domain Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **PACE (C-PACE)** | Property Assessed Clean Energy — public financing for energy-efficient building improvements |
| **Credit Facility** | Formal agreement with a capital provider for access to a pool of capital under pre-agreed terms |
| **Spread** | Margin/fee on top of base interest rate; spread split defines revenue sharing (e.g., 60/40) |
| **Origination** | Process of sourcing, structuring, and funding a new deal |
| **Underwriting** | Evaluating the risk and terms of a potential deal |
